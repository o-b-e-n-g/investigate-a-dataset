Introduction¶

Dataset Description¶

This data set contains information about over 10,000 movies collected from The Movie Database (TMDb) over a period of years from 1960 to 2015. The data set contains information about movies in tables such as the title, casts and director and it also contains information about the perfomance of movies in tables such as budget, revenue and popularity.

Question(s) for Analysis¶

We will seeks to answer questions mainly related to the profitability of the moves. Three questions we would look to answer from this data set are:

  1. Which years do movies have the most profits?
  2. What is the relationship between the profit of movies and the following: 
         a. The Budgets
     b. The Popularity
  3. What are the most profitable genres?

#A function to group by the sum of profit, sort and plot a bar graph is defined

def barplot(dataframe, xlabels, ylabels, barcolor):
    #group the data and select the top 10
    profitable = dataframe.groupby(xlabels)[ylabels].sum().nlargest(10)
    
    #sort the data in descending order
    profitable_sorted = profitable.sort_values(ascending = False)
    
    #describe/create the titles of the plot and style
    profitable_sorted.plot(kind='bar', color= barcolor, label= ylabels, figsize = (10,6));
    plt.xlabel(xlabels);
    plt.ylabel(ylabels + ' in billions (USD)');
    plt.title('most profitable ' + xlabels +'s', fontsize = 14);
    plt.legend();

#A function for the line of best fit is defined

def best_fit(xcolumn, ycolumn):
    #Plotting a linear regression model fit
    lr = sns.regplot(x=df[xcolumn],y=df[ycolumn],color='gold')
    
    #describe/create the titles of the plot and style
    lr.set_xlabel(xcolumn)
    lr.set_ylabel(ycolumn  + ' in billions (USD)')
    lr.set_title(xcolumn +" against " + ycolumn,fontsize=14)
    sns.set(rc={'figure.figsize':(12, 8)})
    sns.set_style("whitegrid")

    correlation = df[xcolumn].corr(df[ycolumn])
    
    print("The correlation between " + xcolumn  + " and " + ycolumn + " is " + str(correlation))

Conclusions¶

This analysis was performed on a data set containing information about over 10,000 movies collected from The Movie Database (TMDb) over a period of years from 1960 to 2015. The data set contains information about movies in tables such as the title, casts and director and it also contains information about the perfomance of movies in tables such as budget, revenue and popularity.

As part of the data wrangling process, certain issues were idientified certain issues which were resolved in the data cleaning process. These issues included:

  1. Dropping duplicated rows
  2. Dropping movies with no budgets or revenues
  3. Dropping irrelevant columns

From our analysis of the dataset, we observed the following:

  1. The year 2015, the most current year as the most profitable year. However, movies/the movie industry had gradually grown more profitable with every new year. cross the top 10 profitable years, there were only dips in profitability from 2009 to 2010 and 2011 to 2012.
  2. Budgets and Profits and Popularity and Profits are both moderately correlated. However, there is a stronger relationship between profits and popularity than between profits and budgets.
  3. Over the span of 55 years, adventure movies made the most profits, followed by action and comedy.
